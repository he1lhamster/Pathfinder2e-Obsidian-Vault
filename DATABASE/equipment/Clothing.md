## Clothing (Ordinary)<span class="item-type">Item 0</span>

**Source** [[DATABASE/source/Core Rulebook|Core Rulebook]] 
**Price** 1 sp
Ordinary clothing is functional with basic tailoring, such as peasant garb, monk’s robes, or work clothes.

## Clothing (Explorer's)<span class="item-type">Item 0</span>

**Source** [[DATABASE/source/Core Rulebook|Core Rulebook]] 
**Price** 1 sp
**Bulk** L
Explorer’s clothing is sturdy enough that it can be reinforced to protect you, even though it isn’t a suit of armor. It comes in many forms, though the most common sorts look like clerical vestments, monk’s garments, or wizard’s robes, as members of all three classes are likely to avoid wearing armor. For more information on explorer’s clothing, see [[DATABASE/armor/Explorer's Clothing|its entry in the armor category]].

## Clothing (Fine)<span class="item-type">Item 0</span>

**Source** [[DATABASE/source/Core Rulebook|Core Rulebook]] 
**Price** 2 gp
**Bulk** L
Fine clothing, suitable for a noble or royal, is made with expensive fabrics, precious metals, and intricate patterns. You can wear only one tool set of light Bulk with fine clothing, instead of the normal limit of 2 Bulk.

## Clothing (High-Fashion Fine)<span class="item-type">Item 3</span>

**Source** [[DATABASE/source/Core Rulebook|Core Rulebook]] 
**Price** 55 gp
**Bulk** L
You gain a +1 item bonus to checks to [[DATABASE/action/Make an Impression|Make an Impression]] on upper-class folk while wearing high-fashion fine clothing.

## Clothing (Winter)<span class="item-type">Item 0</span>

**Source** [[DATABASE/source/Core Rulebook|Core Rulebook]] 
**Price** 4 sp
**Bulk** L
Winter clothing allows you to negate the damage from severe environmental cold and reduce the damage from extreme cold to that of severe cold.
